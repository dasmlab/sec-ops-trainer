# Expert Tier - SecOps Standards Officer

## Overview

The Expert Tier represents the SecOps Standards Officer role. At this level, professionals set security policy, posture, vision, and stance for the organization. They provide strategic input into Security Risk Management Frameworks and influence security practices across all security teams and DevOps organizations.

## Duration
**Ongoing** (continuous learning and evolution)

## Role Characteristics

At this tier, the professional:
- **Sets the tone and north star** for security across the organization
- **No longer** performs day-to-day hands-on implementations (but understands them)
- Provides **strategic leadership** in security standards and policy
- Influences **cross-functional teams** (SecOps, Security, Pen Testing, Vulnerability Management, SecDevOps, DevOps)
- Makes **strategic decisions** on security posture and risk tolerance
- Evolves security vision based on threat landscape and business needs

## Expert Tier Competency Areas

### Domain Expertise (All Domains)

The Standards Officer must maintain expertise across all domains, with deep specialization in key areas:

1. **OSI Layers** - Strategic understanding of network security architecture
2. **Standards Bodies** - Expert-level knowledge in chosen frameworks
3. **Linux/Open Systems** - Strategic perspective on infrastructure security
4. **Networking** - Architecture-level network security understanding
5. **Virtualization/Containers** - Strategic cloud and container security
6. **Hardware/Infrastructure** - Risk-based infrastructure decisions
7. **Identity & Access** - Strategic IAM architecture and policy

### Specialization Areas

While maintaining broad expertise, Standards Officers often specialize in:
- Industry-specific standards (healthcare, finance, government)
- Regional compliance (e.g., ITSG for Canada, GDPR for EU)
- Specific technology domains (cloud, identity, network)
- Risk management methodologies

# Advanced Tier - Policy Development & Risk Frameworks

## Overview

The Advanced Tier transitions from hands-on implementation to strategic policy development, risk management frameworks, and comprehensive security governance. This tier prepares candidates for leadership roles in security standards and policy.

## Duration
**18-24 months** (building on Intermediate Tier)

## Core Competencies

By the end of this tier, candidates should:
- Develop and implement security policies and standards
- Understand and apply risk management frameworks
- Have expertise in virtualization and containerization security
- Understand identity and access management at scale
- Be capable of infrastructure security at scale
- Provide strategic security guidance to cross-functional teams

## 2. Risk Management Frameworks

### Priority: **HIGHEST**

#### Required Knowledge

**NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF):**
- [NIST SP 800-37](https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/detail/sp/800-37/rev-2/final) - Risk Management Framework
- RMF Steps: Prepare, Categorize, Select, Implement, Assess, Authorize, Monitor
- Continuous monitoring
- Authorization processes

**NIST SP 800-53:**
- Security and Privacy Controls
- Control selection and tailoring
- Control baselines
- Assessment procedures

**NIST Cybersecurity Framework:**
- Framework Core (Functions, Categories, Subcategories)
- Implementation Tiers
- Framework Profiles
- Integration with RMF

**ISO 27005:**
- Information Security Risk Management
- Risk assessment methodologies
- Risk treatment options

**FAIR (Factor Analysis of Information Risk):**
- Quantitative risk analysis
- Risk measurement methodologies

#### Training Resources

**ISO Training:**

**Other Resources:**

## 4. Identity & Access Management (IAM)

### Priority: **HIGH**

#### Required Knowledge

**IAM Technologies:**
- **OIDC (OpenID Connect)**
  - OAuth 2.0 foundation
  - ID tokens and access tokens
  - Identity provider concepts
  - Client types and flows

- **SAML (Security Assertion Markup Language)**
  - SAML 2.0 protocol
  - Identity provider (IdP) and service provider (SP)
  - SSO implementations
  - SAML assertions and attributes

- **XACML (eXtensible Access Control Markup Language)**
  - Policy-based access control
  - Policy decision points
  - Attribute-based access control (ABAC)

**Modern IAM Concepts:**
- Zero Trust Architecture
- Privileged Access Management (PAM)
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Single Sign-On (SSO)
- Identity Federation
- Just-In-Time (JIT) access
- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) vs ABAC

#### Training Resources

**Identity Training:**
- [SANS SEC530: Defensible Security Architecture](https://www.sans.org/course/defensible-security-architecture-engineering) (includes IAM)
- OAuth 2.0 and OIDC training
- SAML implementation courses
- Identity and access management certifications

**Red Hat Identity Management:**
- [Red Hat Identity Management (RH362)](https://www.redhat.com/en/services/training/rh362-red-hat-identity-management)
- Red Hat Directory Server
- FreeIPA/IdM training

**Industry Training:**
- Identity provider training (Okta, Azure AD, etc.)
- Zero Trust Architecture training
- PAM solutions training

#### Key Skills
- Design IAM architectures
- Implement SSO solutions (SAML, OIDC)
- Understand modern authentication/authorization patterns
- Assess IAM security posture
- Design privilege access management
- Implement zero trust principles

## Advanced Tier Completion Criteria

### Knowledge Checkpoints
- [ ] Can develop comprehensive security policies
- [ ] Understands and can apply risk management frameworks (NIST RMF)
- [ ] Expertise in virtualization/containerization security
- [ ] Deep IAM knowledge (OIDC, SAML, XACML)
- [ ] Can assess infrastructure security at scale
- [ ] Specialized expertise in 2-3 standards bodies
- [ ] Can influence cross-functional teams

### Certification Milestones
- [ ] **CISSP (Certified Information Systems Security Professional)** - **HIGHLY RECOMMENDED**
- [ ] Lead Auditor/Implementer in chosen standard (ISO, etc.)
- [ ] Advanced certifications in virtualization/cloud (if applicable)
- [ ] RHCA (Red Hat Certified Architect) - Security track - Recommended

### Practical Experience
- [ ] Developed and implemented security policies
- [ ] Led risk management framework implementation
- [ ] Designed secure architectures (virtualization, containers, cloud)
- [ ] Implemented IAM solutions
- [ ] Provided strategic security guidance
- [ ] Influenced cross-functional teams

### Next Steps
Once Advanced Tier is complete, proceed to [Expert Tier](04-expert-tier.md)

## Recommended Learning Order

1. **Risk Management Frameworks** (NIST RMF deep dive)
2. **Policy Development** (SANS leadership courses)
3. **Virtualization/Container Security** (Red Hat + cloud training)
4. **IAM Deep Dive** (OIDC, SAML, XACML)
5. **Standards Specialization** (choose 2-3 for expertise)
6. **Strategic Skills** (communication, influence)
7. **Infrastructure at Scale** (parallel track)

## Time Allocation (24-month path)

- Policy Development & Risk Frameworks: 30% of time
- Virtualization/Container Security: 20% of time
- IAM: 15% of time
- Standards Specialization: 15% of time
- Strategic Skills: 10% of time
- Infrastructure at Scale: 10% of time

# SecOps Standards Officer - Competency Development Plan

## Overview

This repository contains a comprehensive, tiered competency development plan for individuals aspiring to become **SecOps Standards Officers** within an organization. A SecOps Standards Officer is responsible for setting Security Policy, Posture, Vision, and Stance within the company, and provides critical input into Security Risk Management Frameworks.

## Role Definition

The SecOps Standards Officer:
- **Sets the tone and north star** for SecOps, Security, Pen Testing, Vulnerability Management teams
- Influences SecDevOps and DevOps teams in application development security
- **No longer** performs hands-on day-to-day implementations and remediations
- Has **formerly** been hands-on and understands the practical realities
- Focuses on **strategic policy, governance, and risk management**

3. **[Linux & Open Systems](DOMAINS/linux-rhel.md)** - RHEL-focused, practical deployment experience
4. **[Networking & Routing](DOMAINS/networking-routing.md)** - WAN/LAN, Cisco certifications
5. **[Virtualization & Containerization](DOMAINS/virtualization-containers.md)** - PaaS, IaaS, CaaS
6. **[Hardware & Infrastructure at Scale](DOMAINS/hardware-infrastructure.md)** - Chip, platform, hardware risk management

## Key Principles

- **RHEL-First Approach**: Prioritizes Red Hat Enterprise Linux training and certifications
- **Practical Experience**: Emphasizes real-world deployment experience alongside theoretical knowledge
- **Branchable Paths**: Allows specialization while maintaining core competencies
- **Standards-Focused**: Integrates understanding of governing bodies and compliance frameworks
